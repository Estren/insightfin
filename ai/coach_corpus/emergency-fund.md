# The emergency fund

An emergency fund is money set aside exclusively to cover unexpected, urgent expenses — job loss, medical bills, a critical repair — without resorting to high-interest debt. It is the foundation of financial stability and usually the first goal to build.

## How much to save

A common target is **3 to 6 months of essential expenses** — not income:

- **3 months** — stable salaried job, dual-income household, low fixed costs.
- **6 months** — single income, dependents, or higher job volatility.
- **12 months** — freelancers and variable income, or a sole earner in a niche field.

Base the target on *essential* monthly spending (housing, food, transport, utilities, insurance, minimum debt payments), not your full budget.

## Where to keep it

Liquidity and safety beat returns. Keep it where you can access it within a day or two, with minimal risk to the principal: a high-yield savings account, a money-market fund, or an equivalent short-term instrument. It should **not** sit in volatile assets like stocks or crypto — the whole point is that it's there when markets are down.

## How to build it

- **Start small:** even one month of expenses meaningfully changes your risk profile.
- **Automate** a fixed transfer on payday so it's funded before discretionary spending.
- **Redirect windfalls** — bonuses, tax refunds — to reach the target faster.
- Once it's full, **stop contributing** and redirect that flow to longer-term investing.

## When to use it

Only for genuine emergencies: unexpected, necessary, and urgent. A planned purchase or a good sale is not an emergency. After using it, prioritize refilling it before resuming other goals.
